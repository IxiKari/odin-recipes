Har Gow

Dim Sum Shrimp Dumplings

The Dim Sum tables are filled with many plates displaying all sorts of flavors, but one of the most popular items on the table I would say is the "xia jiao" or "har gow" in Cantonese. That might be a little biased, though, because they happen to be one of my favorites. They are these beautiful crystal white dumplings stuffed with steamed shrimp.

This Dim Sum dish is easy to love but difficult to follow

Ingredients

For The Filling

  • 1/2 pound raw shrimp
  • 1 teaspoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots

For The Dough

  • 1 cup of wheat starch
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 1/4 cups of boiling water
  • 3 teaspoons lard

Instructions

For The Filling

  1. Mix everything (except the bamboo) together; whip in one direction for a few minutes until the mixture starts to look sticky. Now add the chopped bamboo shoots, and mix everything together. Cover and refrigerate while preparing the dough.

For the Dough

  1. Mix the wheat starch and cornstarch in a mixing bowl. Slowly add in the boiling water, while stirring rapidly. Now add in the lard (or oil) and continue to stir. Knead the dough for a couple of minutes, until it turns into a smooth dough ball. Roll the dough into a long cylinder, and divide it into 18 equal pieces. Cover the dough pieces with a damp paper towel.

  2. Once the water in the steamer is boiled, steam the shrimp dumplings for 6 minutes using high heat and serve hot. Make sure that they each have an inch and a half to expand during the cooking process.
